## **Problem**

Gen Z and Millennial social media users struggle to disengage from negative content loops ("doomscrolling") on platforms like TikTok and Reddit, often entering a state of "problematic use" where they feel unable to stop despite recognizing the negative impact on their mood and schedule.

---

## **Impact**

* **Mental & Physical Health:** Users experience increased anxiety, depression, and sleep disturbances due to prolonged exposure to distressing content.  
* **Loss of Function:** Doomscrollers are 4x more likely to miss deadlines or life opportunities compared to non-doomscrollers due to distraction.  
* **Loss of Time:** The average user loses 3.5 hours per week (during workdays alone) to doomscrolling, often triggered by political content or "brain rot".

---

## **Evidence**

* **Prevalence:** 64% of Americans describe themselves as doomscrollers; this spikes to 81% for Gen Z.  
* **Frequency:** 43% of Americans doomscroll daily; 26% do so multiple times a day (Morning Consult).  
* **Severity:** A 2024 WHO report found over 10% of adolescents struggle to control their usage, indicating behavior closer to addiction than habit.

---

## **Opportunity**

**OPPORTUNITY STATEMENT:** Enable Gen Z and Millennial "doomscrollers" to interrupt passive consumption loops and immediately convert that time into micro-progress towards personal goals (e.g., learning a skill, fitness), reclaiming an average of 3.5 hours per week for meaningful activity through daily intentional practice.

### **Expected Outcomes**

* **User Outcome:** Users reduce passive screen time by 20% within 30 days and complete at least 1 productive session every day (7-day streak target).  
* **Product Outcome:** Achieve 25% Day-30 retention, proving daily expiration enforcement works where willpower-based apps fail.  
* **Strategic Outcome:** Position the product not just as a "blocker," but as a "daily intentionality coach," differentiating from OS-level restrictions like Apple Screen Time and accumulation-based apps.

---

## **Market Validation (Why Now)**

* **The "Attention Crisis" Peak:** Algorithms have optimized passive consumption to the point of "problematic use" for 10% of adolescents, creating an urgent demand for counter-tools.  
* **The "Brain Rot" Backlash:** 42% of users specifically identify "brain rot" content as a trigger, signaling a cultural readiness to shift away from low-value content.  
* **Political Fatigue:** With 51% of Americans citing politics as a doomscrolling trigger, the current political climate creates a massive, immediate desire for tools that filter out high-stress noise.

## **Users & Needs**

### **WHO:**

#### **Primary Users:**

**The "Powerless Scroller"** \- Gen Z and Millennial social media users (ages 18-35) who recognize they lack self-control with social media apps. They've tried built-in Screen Time limits but always click "Ignore." They want a system that enforces boundaries for them and makes productive time feel rewarding instead of restrictive. They need daily structure because they can't trust themselves to manage accumulated time wisely.

#### **Secondary Users:**

**The "Aspiring Optimizer"** \- Gen Z and Millennial users (ages 22-35) who don't identify as addicted but want to intentionally structure their time. They view the app as a productivity framework that gamifies balance \- treating leisure time as something to be earned and budgeted rather than consumed mindlessly. They're drawn to the quantified self aspect and the psychological satisfaction of "guilt-free" scrolling within daily boundaries.

### **NEEDS:**

#### **Must Have:**

1. **As a Powerless Scroller, I need my vice apps (TikTok, Instagram, Twitter) to be completely blocked by default**, so that I cannot open them unconsciously during moments of boredom or stress and immediately fall into a doomscrolling loop.  
2. **As a Powerless Scroller, I need to see verified proof of my productive time (via iOS Screen Time data) before unlocking vice apps**, so that I trust the system is fair and know I actually earned my leisure time rather than gaming the system. (Note: Aspiring Optimizers value this for the satisfaction of quantified achievement)  
3. **As a Powerless Scroller, I need my earned minutes to work as a shared bank across all my vice apps (not locked to one app)**, so that I have flexibility in how I spend my earned time based on what I actually need in the moment (checking Instagram DMs vs. watching TikTok).  
4. **As a Powerless Scroller, I need the app to automatically track my productive time in native apps (Kindle, Duolingo) without forcing me to use web versions**, so that I can maintain my existing reading/learning habits and earn credit seamlessly in the background.  
5. **As a Powerless Scroller, I need real-time deduction from my time bank as I use vice apps**, so that I feel the immediate "cost" of scrolling and can make conscious decisions about whether to continue or save my remaining minutes for later. (Note: Aspiring Optimizers value this as budget awareness)

#### **Should Have:**

6. **As a Powerless Scroller, I need notifications when I hit productivity milestones (e.g., 15 min, 30 min earned)**, so that I'm reminded of my progress and feel motivated to continue building my time bank rather than giving up.  
7. **As a Powerless Scroller, I need my earned time to expire when the day ends at midnight**, so that I'm forced to start each day fresh rather than using accumulated time from yesterday to doomscroll first thing in the morning. This prevents binge-earning patterns and reinforces daily intentional practice.

#### **Nice to Have:**

8. **As an Aspiring Optimizer, I need to adjust my productive-to-vice app ratio (e.g., 2:1 instead of 1:1)**, so that I can increase difficulty as I build better habits over time and challenge myself further.

## **Proposed Solution**

**PROPOSED SOLUTION:**

A time-trading iOS app featuring a supportive accountability buddy (Clepsy) that blocks distracting social media apps by default. The app only unlocks these "vice apps" when users earn equivalent time through productive activities tracked via Apple's Screen Time API. Clepsy uses a functional hourglass body to visualize the user‚Äôs balance and goal progress, making enforcement feel collaborative rather than punitive.

### **TOP 3 MVP VALUE PROPS:**

1. **The Vitamin (Must-Have Baseline):**  
    **Verified blocking that can't be bypassed** \- Your vice apps (TikTok, Instagram) are locked by iOS until you earn access, eliminating the "Ignore Limit" button that makes other blocking apps useless for people with zero self-control.  
2. **The Painkiller (Core Problem Solver):**  
    **Force conscious decisions, not autopilot scrolling** \- Every time you try to open TikTok, you see exactly what it costs (23 minutes of productive time), breaking the unconscious habit loop that turns "just a quick check" into 2-hour doomscrolling sessions you never intended.  
3. **The Steroid (Magic Moment):**  
    **Every day is a fresh start** \- Your time bank resets at midnight, eliminating the ability to coast on yesterday's productivity. This forces daily intentionality and prevents the "binge and coast" pattern that sabotages long-term habit change. You can't game the system by banking hours on Sunday to doomscroll guilt-free on Monday morning.

## **Goals & Non-Goals**

### **GOALS:**

1. **Break the autopilot doomscrolling habit** \- Enable users to reduce unintentional vice app usage by 50% within 30 days by forcing conscious unlock decisions instead of mindless opening.  
2. **Drive daily productive behavior** \- Users complete at least 1 productive session every day (7-day streak target within first 30 days), preventing binge-earn patterns and reinforcing daily intentional practice.  
3. **Achieve product-market fit with retention** \- Reach 25% Day-30 retention among Powerless Scrollers, proving the enforcement mechanism works where willpower-based apps fail.  
4. **Validate core mechanic before monetization** \- Gather qualitative feedback and usage data to inform future premium features. Launch free to remove barriers to testing the time-trading concept.

### **NON-GOALS:**

1. **Not building buddy/social features in MVP** \- No password-protected unlocks, shared leaderboards, or friend challenges. These are V1.5 features pending core value validation.  
2. **Not supporting Android** \- iOS-only for MVP due to Screen Time API dependency. Android requires different technical approach (accessibility services), delayed to V2.  
3. **Not offering custom exchange rates** \- MVP is strict 1:1 time trading (1 min productive \= 1 min vice). Variable ratios (2:1, etc.) are V2 difficulty settings.  
4. **Not building in-app productivity tools** \- No web browser for Kindle, no built-in journal, no meditation timer. Users must use their native apps.  
5. **Not offering time rollover or banking** \- MVP enforces daily expiration at midnight. Weekend rollover or accumulation features are V1.5/V2 considerations pending user feedback.  
6. **Not monetizing at launch** \- Free for all users to maximize adoption and validate core mechanics before introducing paid tiers.

* #### **\[P0\] System Monitoring:** Passive tracking is handled via `DeviceActivityMonitor` and runs in the background even when Clepsy is closed.

## **Technical Dependencies**

### **iOS Frameworks Required:**

* **FamilyControls** \- One-time permission request for Screen Time management  
* **ManagedSettings** \- Shield/unshield vice apps programmatically  
* **DeviceActivity** \- Monitor app usage and set threshold-based triggers  
* **DeviceActivityReport** \- Display usage data within app interface

### **Minimum Requirements:**

* **iOS Version:** iOS 16+ (DeviceActivityReport improvements)  
* **Device:** iPhone only (iPad support V2)

### **Key Technical Constraints:**

* DeviceActivityReport updates approximately every 1 minute (not real-time)  
* Usage data is tokenized and cannot be exported off-device for privacy  
* Shields persist across device restarts (handled by iOS)  
* Midnight reset requires scheduled DeviceActivity event with timezone awareness

### **Daily Expiration Implementation:**

* Use DeviceActivity schedule to trigger midnight reset  
* Handle timezone changes (travel scenarios)  
* Preserve balance calculation accuracy across device restarts  
* No background notifications before expiration (users discover organically)

   * If users complain about being kicked out mid-TikTok at 12:00 AM, we can revisit in V1.5.  
6. **Emergency Override Mechanism:** How to handle legitimate emergencies (urgent DM, etc.)?  
   * **DEFER TO POST-LAUNCH:** Don't include in MVP. If users request it, add in V1.5 with significant friction:  
     * Requires typing long phrase: "I understand this defeats the purpose of the app"  
     * 24-hour cooldown period  
     * Breaks earning streak  
   * Philosophy: Real emergencies are rare. Most "emergencies" are rationalizations.
